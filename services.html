Legal Queries Answered

Before you hire a lawyer, clear your doubts. Here are answers to the most common questions asked by clients in Uttar Pradesh.

How much does a lawyer charge for a Bail application in UP?

Legal fees in Uttar Pradesh vary based on the advocate's experience and the court level. For a bail application in a District/Sessions Court (like Etawah or Kanpur), fees generally range from 5,000 to 25,000 per hearing or as a lump sum. For High Court bail matters in Lucknow/Allahabad, senior counsels may charge significantly higher. On UP-Legal Directory, you negotiate fees directly with the advocate; we do not charge any commission.

Can I get a Mutual Consent Divorce in less than 6 months?

Generally, Section 13B of the Hindu Marriage Act mandates a 6-month "Cooling-Off Period" between the First and Second Motion. However, following recent Supreme Court judgments (e.g., Amardeep Singh v. Harveen Kaur), the Family Court has the discretion to waive off this period if the parties have already lived apart for over 18 months and mediation has failed. Your lawyer needs to file a specific waiver application for this.

What documents are needed to file a Cheque Bounce case?

To file a complaint under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, you need: 1) The original bounced Cheque, 2) The Bank Return Memo (stating "Insufficient Funds"), 3) Copy of the Legal Notice sent to the debtor, 4) Proof of delivery of notice (Postal Receipt), and 5) An affidavit stating the debt was legally enforceable.

Is online legal consultation valid in court?

Consultation is for advice, not court representation. However, you can hire a lawyer online through UP-Legal Directory, discuss your case via video call/WhatsApp, and send documents digitally. For court hearings (filing bail bonds, cross-examination), your physical presence or your lawyer's presence in the respective court (e.g., Agra District Court) is mandatory as per procedure.
