Legal Education Series

Know The Procedure Before You Hire

We believe an informed client is a powerful client. Here are simplified guides to common legal procedures in Uttar Pradesh.

1 How to File an FIR?

  1. Visit Jurisdiction Thana

    Go to the Police Station (Thana) that covers the area of the crime. If urgent, go to ANY Thana for a 'Zero FIR'.

  2. Written Complaint

    Submit an application. Mention Date, Time, Location, and Suspects clearly. Keep a photocopy.

  3. Get FIR Copy (Free)

    Police must give you a copy of the FIR free of cost under Section 154(2) CrPC.

  4. If Police Refuse?

    Send complaint via Regd. Post to SP. If no action, hire a lawyer to file 156(3) CrPC Application in Court.

2 The Bail Procedure

  1. Check Offense Type

    Is it Bailable (Right) or Non-Bailable (Court Discretion)? Your lawyer checks the IPC sections.

  2. File Application

    Advocate files Bail Application in Magistrate or Sessions Court. Police is asked for the Case Diary.

  3. Arguments & Order

    Lawyer argues why custody is not needed. If satisfied, Judge grants bail. If rejected, move to High Court.

  4. Bail Bonds & Release

    Two sureties (Guarantors) submit FD/RC papers to court. Release order (Parwana) goes to Jail.

Legal Dictionary

Terms You Will Hear in Court

Vakalatnama

A document authorizing an advocate to represent a client in court.

Affidavit

A written statement confirmed by oath or affirmation, for use as evidence in court.

Bail Bond

A document signed by sureties guaranteeing the accused will appear in court.

Injunction

A judicial order (Stay Order) that restrains a person from doing a specific action.

Decree

The formal expression of a final judgment by a Civil Court determining rights.

Caveat

A notice preventing a court from passing orders without hearing the filer first.

Alimony

Financial support paid to a spouse during or after divorce proceedings.

Charge Sheet

Final report by police accusing someone of a crime after investigation.
